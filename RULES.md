# Rules, Eligibility, and Legal Terms

_Last updated: February 2026_

## Organizer

This task is organized by **Pangea Chat, LLC**, a Virginia-based company funded by the U.S. National Science Foundation. We're a small team building AI-powered language learning tools, and we're excited to find our next teammate through this assessment.

We received over 2,200 applications for this role. This skills-based task exists because we wanted a process that evaluates what you can do, not where you went to school. We want the process to be transparent, fair, and respectful of your time.

## Eligibility

To participate, you must meet **all** of the following:

1. **Work authorization**: You must be authorized to work in the United States at the time of the internship (June–August 2026). Pangea Chat does not sponsor employment visas.
2. **Location**: You must be available to work **on-site in Richmond, Virginia** for 10 consecutive weeks during Summer 2026.
3. **Language**: Professional proficiency in written and spoken English.
4. **Education**: Currently enrolled in, or graduated within the past 12 months from, an undergraduate or graduate program in computer science, software engineering, or a related field, or equivalent demonstrated experience.
5. **Age**: You must be at least 18 years old at the time of submission.

**Preference**: While not a requirement, preference will be given to candidates who are interested in full-time employment in Richmond, Virginia following the internship.

## What This Is and Is Not

- This is a **skills-based hiring assessment**, not a contest, sweepstakes, or lottery.
- There is **no entry fee** and **no purchase necessary**.
- Selection is based entirely on **merit** as evaluated by the scoring criteria described in the README.
- Participation in this task does not guarantee an interview or employment.

## Top 10 Finalists

The **top 10 scoring candidates** (as determined by the automated and manual scoring rubric) will each receive:

- A **final-round interview** for the Summer 2026 internship
- With your permission, a **personal LinkedIn recommendation** from the Pangea Chat founder recognizing the quality of your work, regardless of whether you receive an offer

## Submission Rules

1. **Individual work**: Submissions must be your own. You may use any tools you like (see AI Use below), but the submission should represent your thinking.
2. **One submission per person**: Multiple submissions from the same person will be disqualified. You may update your fork up until the deadline.
3. **Deadline**: You will have **one full week** (7 days) from the date the task is distributed. Submissions must be pushed to your public GitHub fork by the communicated deadline. Late submissions will not be evaluated.
4. **Public repo**: Your fork must be publicly accessible at the time of evaluation.
5. **No plagiarism**: Submissions that are substantially copied from another candidate will result in disqualification of all involved parties.

## AI Use

**AI tools are fully allowed.** Use ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, Cursor, or whatever else you want. We'd be hypocrites to say otherwise: your submission will be scored in part by an LLM.

That said: it is unlikely that a purely AI-generated submission will land in the top 10. Here's why. The task requires you to synthesize three things at once: the technical spec, the experience of a real language learner using your feedback, and the sensibility to write code and docs that a human reviewer actually enjoys reading. That kind of synthesis comes from the sprawling, messy context of lived experience that your wet brain is uniquely good at.

Use AI to move faster. Use your judgment to move in the right direction.

## Intellectual Property

- You **retain ownership** of your submission.
- Your submission is hosted on a public GitHub repository. Pangea Chat will review your submission for evaluation purposes.

### Prior Art & Independent Development

The task you're completing (building an LLM-powered language feedback API) **is based on a feature Pangea Chat already ships in production.** We designed this task to reflect real work, which means there will naturally be overlap between your submission and our existing codebase.

We want to be straightforward about what this means:

1. **We built this first.** Pangea Chat's language feedback, grammar checking, and structured LLM output features were developed independently and well before this assessment. Any similarities between your submission and our products come from that prior work, not from yours.
2. **Your work is yours.** You retain full ownership of your submission (see above). We will not use your code in our products.
3. **We won't stop building.** We will continue developing our language feedback features. If our future work resembles approaches in your submission, that's a reflection of the shared problem space, not copying.

The practical upshot: don't worry that we're going to "steal your idea." We already had it. And don't worry that we'll claim your code. It stays yours.
